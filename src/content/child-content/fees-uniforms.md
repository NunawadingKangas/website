### Discounts

- Discount for Under 8 players: Our Under 8 players pay only the $55 registration and $240/$215 team sheet fees amounts of the Nunawading Kangas fees. Their $105 training fees are covered by the Club.

- Discount for non-training teams - these players do not pay the $105 training fee.

- Discount for Monday Girls teams - $30 discount from the registration to encourage particatipation.

- *Sibling discount: A 5% discount applies to Nunawading Kangas fees for 2nd and subsequent siblings registered for the same competition, by the same PlayHQ account holder in PlayHQ.*

**Note: Basketball Victoria and Basketball Australia fees are only paid once a year.**

## Explanation of the fee components

1. Registration fees paid to Nunawading Kangas. These fees cover the Club costs including the costs of trophies, Club administration costs and Association registration fees.

2. Training fees paid to Nunawading Kangas. These fees cover the cost of leasing training venues for our teams. Friday teams do not usually train and so those players do not pay this fee. For Friday teams that wish to train, players will be required to pay an additional fee to cover the cost of the training venue for the season. This will be followed up by the Club at the beginning of the season.

3. Team Sheet Fees contribution. Each team pays a `team sheet` fee per game to the Association. Players from the team each pay a contribution towards these fees for the season. The amount of the weekly "team sheet fee" is set by the Association and by venue owners to cover venue, competition and referee costs. This season the Club will be collecting these contributions with registration and paying the fees on behalf of our teams, directly to the Association.

4. Basketball Victoria Participant Licence. Basketball Victoria charges an annual $29 for juniors (17 and under) or $43 for seniors. These fees help cover the costs of managing the sport statewide, which includes the providing of services and insurance to participants, clubs and associations.

5. Basketball Austrialia Paticipant Fee (Game Development Levy) - new in 2024. $5 (plus GST) annual fee, collected by Basketball Australia - the revenue generated will play a pivotal role in sustaining the growth of basketball.

We encourage you to contact the Regsitrar at Registrar@nunawadingkangas.com.au if fee payment up front is an issue your family. It's important to us that kids don't miss out on playing because financial issues.

For more information about joining Nunawading Kangas please contact our Registrar - Registrar@nunawadingkangas.com.au

## Get Active Kids Vouchers

We are able to accept Get Active Kids Vouchers through our association with Basketball Victoria. As each round of vouchers opens, eligibile families are able to either obtain a voucher through Sports and Recreation Victoria priro to completing their PlayHQ registration and use the voucher code provided when registering, or aply to SRV to be reimbursed for fees already paid in the voucher period.

To be eligible players must be 0-18 years old named on a Health Care Card or Pension Concession Card and Medicare Card. Please use Baskeball Victoria (provider number 2951096) as the provider in your voucher application.

For more details on this program please go to [https://www.getactive.vic.gov.au/vouchers/apply-for-vouchers/](https://www.getactive.vic.gov.au/vouchers/apply-for-vouchers/)

## Uniforms

Please note that unless you have a singlet number clash, the club does not provide loan uniforms. For new members, please ensure you order your uniforms as soon as you register as it can take a while for the uniforms to be sent.

If you need a singlet for training or competition, we encourage you to ask around fellow members — someone may have a spare you can borrow.

For grading (first 6 rounds) refs are a lot more lenient which means you can wear any yellow singlet and use black tape to tape a number on. Black shorts, no pockets. However, after grading games, players MUST have the proper playing uniforms otherwise team points will be deducted as pentalty.

We'd like to take a moment to remind everyone that our club is run entirely by volunteers who dedicate their time and effort to ensure things run smoothly for all members. While we understand that issues and frustrations can occasionally arise, please remember that our volunteers deserve to be treated with courtesy and respect at all times.

We're all here because we share a passion for this club and its community. Let's continue to foster a positive, supportive environment where everyone feels valued.

## Uniform Order Process

All players are required to purchase a playing uniform (singlet and shorts) to participate in games. Given the timeframe for manufacturing the uniforms, players need to ensure they order their uniforms ASAP.

Below is the link to order uniforms and merchandise from the club's official apparel supplier:

[Impact Sports Kangas Club Shop](https://www.impactsports.com.au/club/kangas/)

Please note:

To streamline things, we'll now be allocating singlet numbers to players for the life of the club.

- Existing players will retain the number listed in PlayHQ.
- New players will be assigned a number upon joining.

We try to ensure availability across a player's age group, not just the team they are playing in.

The club does not facilitate a second hand uniform operation but should you acquire a second hand uniform, you will need to contact the Uniform Co-ordinator to check that the singlet number is available and if so, register it to the player before it can be worn in games.

Uniform order cycles end on the 15th of each month and take approximately 4-5 weeks to be manufactured.
